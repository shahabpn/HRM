# HRM Project Overview

## What Is HRM?

HRM (Human Relationship Manager) is a web application for real estate agents that helps them systematically nurture relationships with their existing contacts to generate predictable referral business. It is NOT a CRM — it's the relationship layer that sits alongside any CRM.

**Working title:** HRM (internal). Product needs a branded market-facing name (TBD).

## The Problem

Established real estate agents (5+ years) have databases full of people who already know and trust them, but no system to stay meaningfully connected. Past clients list with other agents because the agent wasn't top of mind. Income is unpredictable. Referrals happen by accident, not by design. Traditional CRMs track transactions, not relationship depth.

## The Solution

HRM operationalizes proven referral methodologies (the 3R Framework, Lifetime Referral System, Mackay 66) into three integrated systems:

1. **Contact Depth Engine** — Progressive profiling based on Harvey Mackay's 66-point framework. Agents build deep knowledge about each contact over time. Gamified with badges and a relationship depth score.

2. **Cadence Engine** — Structures 27–36 meaningful touchpoints per year. Generates a personalized annual calendar. Surfaces daily action items via a Kanban board. Cadence varies by contact segment AND relationship tier.

3. **ROI Dashboard** — Tracks database return rate (transactions per 100 contacts/year). Makes the invisible work of relationship-building measurable and accountable.

## Core Frameworks

### The 3R Framework
Every feature serves one or more pillars:
- **Relevant** — Personalized, timely communications based on deep knowledge
- **Remarkable** — Delivering unexpected value that makes people talk about you
- **Remembered** — Consistent visibility to maintain top-of-mind presence

### Relationship Levels
- **Level 1: Satisfied** — May or may not use you again, unlikely to refer
- **Level 2: Loyal** — Will use you again, but referrals aren't reliable
- **Level 3: Advocacy** — Raving fans who actively spread the word

### The Referral Math
For every 100 advocates: target 10–20 transactions/year = 10–20% database return rate.
